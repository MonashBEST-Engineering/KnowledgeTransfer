# 📝 Recruitment Process Documentation

## Purpose

This document outlines the full recruitment process for bringing new members into the team. It covers form distribution, application review, interview handling, and onboarding. It is designed to be simple, consistent, and effective at identifying applicants with the right mindset and problem-solving ability.

---

## Step 1: Distribute the Application Form

📄 **Form link:**  
https://forms.gle/wiyMZXL69xHLm4bHA

- This is the only valid form. Do not accept applications submitted by any other means.
- The form has required fields to prevent incomplete submissions. Anyone who manages to submit false, incomplete, or malformed information (e.g., invalid email addresses or incorrect student IDs) should be rejected immediately.
- However, if someone seems like a strong applicant but made a small error in their student ID or email, you can manually reach out and ask them to re-submit with correct information. Only do this if it’s genuinely worth your time.
- Distribute the form through channels that make sense: student groups, Discords, societies, mailing lists, etc.

---

## Step 2: Review Applications in the Application Manager

📊 **Spreadsheet link:**  
https://docs.google.com/spreadsheets/d/1AHPt3z13-lI5XX-mwfD_9bwHHo0h-SZMwV2czh99b_Q/edit?gid=0#gid=0

- All application data is automatically collected here.
- There is no formal rubric for selecting candidates. Use your judgment. If someone looks promising, give them an interview. If not, reject them. The interview matters far more than the form responses.

---

## Step 3: Interviews (Automated Scheduling + Online Only)

- Once you mark someone for interview, they’ll automatically receive an email asking them to send their availability for the upcoming week. You do not need to send this manually.
- All interviews must be conducted online. Use Microsoft Teams or Zoom. Do not offer in-person interviews—they take too long and aren’t worth the effort.

### Use of Interview Slides

🎞️ **Slide deck link:**  
https://docs.google.com/presentation/d/1Pk-AGpuDps825OyoIoATBPtpFLhy8rc1oLZP5PKqVjw/edit?usp=drive_link

- This slide deck is used in every interview, regardless of the applicant’s role. The purpose of the interview is not to assess technical expertise alone but to evaluate the applicant’s problem-solving ability and character.
- **Important:** You are not required to make candidates answer every technical question in the slides. Use your discretion. Focus on discussion, adaptability, and how they approach unfamiliar problems.
- You can run either individual or group interviews, depending on how availability aligns. There is no issue with scheduling conflicts because you're in control of the format.

---

## Step 4: Rejections Are Automated

- If an applicant is rejected—whether before or after the interview—the system will automatically send them a rejection email. You don’t need to do anything. Don’t waste time with custom emails or explanations.

---

## Step 5: Accepting Candidates

Once you're confident in accepting someone, follow this exact order:

1. **Manually add them to the Microsoft Teams workspace.**  
   This is your responsibility as the person accepting them.

2. **Manually add them to Google Drive.**  
   This is your responsibility as the person accepting them.

3. **Mark them as “Accepted” in the Application Manager.**  
   Once marked, their information will be automatically added to the member list.

> If you skip the Teams and Drive step, they won’t get access to anything. Don’t mark someone as accepted until that’s done.

---

## Final Notes

- Use the tools provided—don’t make up new processes.
- Do not accept or manually process side applications or casual DMs.
- Keep things online and structured.
- Respect your time: don’t manually message people the system already handles.
- Character and problem-solving matter more than textbook answers.
