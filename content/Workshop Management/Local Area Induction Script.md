# Access Requirements

You are allowed to enter this space if you:

  > **Are a current Monash student and have not finished your studies and been course completed.**
  > If you are a current member of MBEST and if you have card access.

**If you do not have card access but need access to the space, please speak to Makerspace staff.**

Core opening hours for the Makerspace currently are **8:00 am to 6:00 pm** weekdays, with extended operating hours offered between **6pm - 9pm** weekdays and **10am - 3pm on Saturdays**. These extended hours will be confirmed with teams on a regular basis.

If you need to access this space outside of these hours for lost property, please contact security.

Never prop open any doors to this building. This is a security concern.

# Procedures for Entering and Exiting 

To enter during opening hours for Makerspace, only a card scan is needed. Do not let non-inducted people into the makerspace if they try to follow you in, as they can pose a safety and security risk. We also do not allow any person who has not completed the Monash Boring Excavation Student Team General Area and Workshop induction into our workspace for the same reasons.

No food or drink is allowed in the Monash Boring Excavation Student Team General Area, and must be taken to the kitchen or kept in bags. Please consume food or drink in the kitchen area.

Bags must be kept under the benches inside the tubs, and must not be placed on the walkways as they can pose a tripping hazard. 

> Inductor to indicate the location of bag tubs now.

Closed toed shoes must be worn at all times within the Makerspace

Safety glasses and hearing protection are not required at all times within the MBEST workarea, but are required for certain approved activities. Safety glasses and hearing protection can be found outside the Composites bay or within our PPE drawer. 

> Inductor to indicate location of the PPE drawer. 

If the work you are conducting requires PPE, let the people around you know as they will either need to vacate the area or wear the same level of PPE themselves.

Please leave your work area clean and tidy before exiting the space.
Alert management if the space is left in a messy state.

Please respond immediately to any requests from Makerspace staff to move equipment or clean a work area, regardless of whether you made the mess or not.

Stop-Work Orders may be imposed on teams whose spaces are not regularly cleaned and maintained.

# Emergency Responses 

There are no safety showers in this area. Eye wash is available at the entrance between the electrical and fabrication areas. Eye flush saline solution is also available in the first aid kits throughout the space. One on the East wall of compactus and one just to the right of the roller door.

Fire extinguishers are distributed around the space. The nearest one is near the roller door.

The closest emergency power emergency stop is to next to the MBEST's workshop, on the wall. 

> Inductor to indicate the position of the e-stop

A list of Makerspace first aiders can be found on the Makerspace Contacts sheet located above the first aid kits. If you require first aid, speak to a Makerspace supervisor or a qualified team member. First aid kits are located on walls near the compactus (G36), in a cabinet in the kitchen (G39) and in the open area near the folding door (G33B).

If you require an ambulance call 000 and then call Monash Security who can direct the ambulance.

Nearest Defib is located outside of the kitchen above the drinking taps.

Please familiarize yourselves with the emergency procedures diagram and the assembly point, which is the basketball court to the south of the building. All doors in the space are emergency exits, please leave through the nearest door.

Please note in addition to the standard warning and evacuation alarms this room is also fitted with lights to draw attention in case there is loud work being performed. Yellow means warning, prepare to evacuate, Red means evacuate now.


# Hazards Present in the Work Area

- No Biological hazards

- No poisons or drugs in this space. You are not to be in this space if you are under the influence of drugs and/or alcohol.

- No ionising radiation

- No Cryogenics

- No Nano-tech or Nano-materials

- There are some chemical hazards present in the team area. Be aware that small amounts of glues, solvents, lubricants etc are allowed to be used in this area and only on the tables, not the JBS Cabinets.

  > Note that power in this building is supplied along overhead guide wires. Please do not pull down on the droppers to disconnect any leads -- always support the outlets with both hands

# Risk Management Requirements of Laboratory Users

pertaining to MBEST's workshop space must be followed at all times.
These are safety documents that outline the safe way to conduct work and

available on SARAH, and SDSs can be found on Chemwatch.

**MBEST TBM Operation**

- Don't try to set up or operate the TBM, or any associated sections if you don't know how to.

- If you're unsure of anything, ask someone more experienced.

- There is currently no formal induction procedure for setup or operation. If you want to learn, talk to people in the workshop and they'll show you how. However this is set to change very shortly.

**Use of Tools**

- If you haven't used a tool before don't use it, ask someone for help

- If working with cutting tools (including files, drills etc.) or rivet gun always wear eye safety goggles.

- Always clamp your materials securely to a bench before using any tools.

- If working with power tools, Tie back long hair and secure loose clothing (hoodie strings, sleeves)

- If you are within two metres of someone working and using PPE, you need to be wearing it too.

- Advise others in the workshop of any noisy work you will perform, and offer them Hearing PPE.

- Follow all Makerspace-owned risk assessments when using tools  
  > (Refer to Section 4 for extra details on RAs/SWIs)

**Use of the Compactus**

- Most big, bulky or lesser used equipment is stored in compactus

- If there is something you need from one of the upper shelves, use either of the step ladders located the sides of the compactus

- All compactus is roughly divided by subteams (Automation, Structures, Propulsion and Power Systems).

  - Shelves will be labelled with their intended use
  - If you have anything specific that shouldn't be moved, please write your name, email and a "DO NOT MOVE" note on it

  > Inductor to show inductees the compactus and indicate the purpose of each shelf

**Workshop Cleaning**

- Keep your workspace tidy.

- If doing metalworking/woodworking, clean up any scraps/ filings using the dust pan and brush

- If you remove a tool or piece of equipment from the compactus or JBS drawers, please put it back once you have finished with it.

- Do not leave expensive equipment unattended. This includes power tools, laptops, soldering iron, loose electrical components etc.

- If you are the last to leave the workshop, do a quick check to ensure things are left orderly and safe. This may include:

  - Batteries E-stopped and returned to the Battery Boxes

  - Tools and equipment returned to JBS drawers

  - Chemicals returned to the dangerous goods cabinets

  - If we have borrowed Makerspace equipment, it's returned to its correct location or placed in the boomerang box to return during staffed hours.

  - Any electronic equipment (ie. power supply, heat gun, soldering iron) is switched off and unplugged.

**Workshop Etiquette and Behaviour**

The makerspace often gets tours from potential sponsors and other important members of faculty, so we need to keep the workshop behaviour professional, especially when these tours occur

Not only does the Faculty use the Student Teams to promote Monash Engineering, but other teams use Makerspace Tours to try and appeal to sponsors

Please make sure we are prioritising TBM work in the workshop. If there is any TBM work happening, please make sure to give them the space and resources they need.
