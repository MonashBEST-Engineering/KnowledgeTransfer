# Getting to SARAH Event Risk Management Plan

Safety and Risk Analysis Hub can be accessed through the [following link](https://www.monash.edu/hsw/sarah).
Select **Event Risk Management Plan (ERMP)**.
This opens the register where your Event Risk Management Plans will appear.

# Creating an Event Risk Management Plan

Click **New** in the top bar.

Enter the name and description of the event.
The name should be simple and clearly identifiable.
An example name is *Team O Week Event*.
Enter a description of the event in the field provided.

Select the type of event.
Examples of event types include conferences, work retreats, gallery opening nights, student or team competitions, student–staff barbecues, clubs and society functions, festivals, and digital events that occur only through Zoom or other online tools.
On-campus major events apply when more than 3,000 people are participating per day.
On-campus standard events apply when less than 3,000 people per day are anticipated.
Other options include off-campus events, research projects involving multiple activities, or “Other” for situations such as participating in a conference or symposium where Monash staff are staffing an exhibition kiosk.

Enter the event start date and end date.

Enter the Event Risk Management Plan approver.
This person is the head of unit or the most senior person responsible for the event.
Fill in their name, email and contact number.
Enter the communications person, who is the emergency contact for unexpected issues.
This may be the approver, a direct supervisor, or another appropriate contact.
Tick the checkbox confirming that this person has been notified prior to the event.

Select **Next**.

# Activity Selection and Risk Assessments

Select the activities that will occur at your event using the drop-down lists.
Examples include Catering and Food and Alcohol.

Minimum Requirements risk assessments will automatically populate.
When you create the associated risk assessment in the WHS Risk Module, ensure the Activities section is ticked so these requirements link correctly.

To view a risk assessment, select the magnifying glass.
A new tab will open showing the task description, purpose of the assessment, referenced documents, the detailed description of risks, minimum control measures, and any other requirements.
Close the tab when finished.

Continue selecting all activity categories relevant to the event.
Examples include emergency preparedness, first aid, equipment use, outdoor activities, temporary structures, pandemic-related measures and travel activities.
Associated Minimum Requirements documents must be selected.

If additional risk assessments are required, select **Find** and search by risk ID or browse the list.
These appear under *Directly Linked Risk Assessments* and you may add as many as needed.

If no existing assessment applies, select **Create** to develop a new risk assessment and attach it.

Tick the confirmation box to indicate that the Event Risk Management Plan Leader has read and understood each risk assessment and ensured that the control measures are in place and communicated to participants.

Select **Next**.

# Event Team

This page lists the event team.
The first box automatically fills with the details of the person completing the plan; replace it if this person is not the Event Risk Management Plan Leader.

Enter an emergency contact name and number for a backup person who can be reached if the leader is not contactable.

Add all event team participants and assign roles through the dropdown list.

Events with more than 10 attendees must have at least one Event Safety Marshal.
There must be one marshal for every 500 attendees.
Enter all event safety marshals, event first aiders, COVID check-in marshals and anyone assisting during the event.

Enter the overall total number of participants.
A range may be used but should reflect expected attendance accurately.

Select **Next**.

# Locations, Times and Highest-Risk Activity

Enter the locations, times and highest-risk activity.
Simple events may only require a single location.
Complex or overnight events may require a sequence of locations.

For recurring events such as school groups visiting campus, the Event Risk Management Plan can be cloned and submitted each time.

An example entry is travel from Monash Clayton Campus from 9:00am to 10:00am.
The system will display the direct route.

Enter the reporting method.
If no contact is required, select the appropriate option.
If check-ins are required, indicate when contact must be made (before activities, during activities or at the conclusion).
Field trips may require daily or hourly safety check-ins depending on the relevant risk assessment.

Continue documenting the entire event schedule.

Select **Next**.

# Additional Information and Attachments

Enter any additional information such as vehicles used during travel.
Attach documents such as contracts, agreements or medical certificates.
Use the Notes section for record keeping or team-specific information.

Select **Next**.

# Finalisation and Submission

If you are not ready to submit, select **Previous** and save the plan as a draft.
Drafts appear in the register under **My Drafts**, and you may edit them using the pen icon, invite others to view them or complete a peer review.

When all information is complete, submit the plan to the approver.
The plan may be printed or saved as a PDF.
Conduct an OHS briefing with the event team before the event.

The submitted plan will appear as **New** until the approver takes action.
Once approved, the status changes to **Approved**, and preparation for the event can proceed.

# Starting and Completing the Event

When ready to begin the event, click the airplane icon to notify the team that the event has commenced.

When the event is complete, click **Return** to archive the Event Risk Management Plan.

For more information on risk assessments, refer to the Risk Assessment Help Training through MyDevelopment or attend the OHS Risk Assessment Training.
